What This Means

The Reversal

Trump's signal is deliberately ambiguous — "at appropriate time" could mean anything from days to never. Markets are trading hope, not certainty. This is a sentiment-driven rally on the thinnest of pretexts. The fundamental picture hasn't changed: Iran has a new hardliner Supreme Leader, 7 Americans are dead, and NATO just intercepted a missile over a member state.

Oil's Wild Ride

$67 → $119 → $88 in 10 days. That's not a market — it's a battlefield. Physical supply destruction doesn't reverse with a tweet. Iraq output is down 70%, refineries are offline, shipping lanes are disrupted. Even if a ceasefire happened tomorrow, it would take months to normalize supply chains. The $88 print is hope-priced, not reality-priced.

CPI Wednesday — The Real Test

Here's the trap: February CPI measures inflation before oil doubled. If inflation was already running hot pre-war, then stagflation is confirmed even if the war ends tomorrow. Energy costs haven't even begun filtering through to the data yet. A hot print would be catastrophic for the "Fed will save us" narrative. This is genuinely the most important number of the year.

The Fed Trap

Powell is in a box. Can't cut — inflation is surging with oil at $88+ and supply chains breaking. Can't hike — the economy is absorbing a war-driven demand shock and recession risk is mounting. The FOMC meets March 17-18. Wednesday's CPI decides which direction the Fed leans. If it's hot, expect hawkish hold + forward guidance that kills the rally. If it's cool, the relief rally could have legs — but don't bet on it.

🧠 What This Means

Why Oil Breaking $100 Matters

Oil hasn't been here since 2022. But this time it's different — the Strait of Hormuz (20% of global oil) is effectively shut, Iranian refineries are burning, and the world's largest LNG facility just went offline. This isn't a speculative spike, it's a physical supply shock.

The Stagflation Trap

Wednesday's CPI is the hinge point. If inflation was already rising before oil hit $107, the Fed is trapped: raise rates (kill growth) or hold (let inflation run). Oil at $107+ flows through to gas prices in 2-3 weeks, food in 4-6 weeks. Every $10/barrel adds ~$0.25/gallon at the pump.

LNG Crisis — The Hidden Bomb

Everyone's watching oil, but the LNG crisis may be worse. Qatar's Ras Laffan produced 30% of global LNG. With it offline and no spare capacity anywhere, EU gas is already +60%. Europe's energy security just got a second crisis on top of the Russia situation.

Monday's Open: Brace for Impact

Futures are already pricing in pain: S&P -1.6%, Nasdaq -1.1%, VIX near panic at 29.49. Oil gapping from $91→$107 over the weekend means Monday's open will be volatile. Watch for forced selling if VIX crosses 30. Gold pulled back from ATH as margin calls force liquidation — classic crisis pattern.

The Water Angle Nobody's Talking About

Iran's strikes on Gulf desalination plants is existential. Bahrain, UAE, and others depend on desalinated water for 90%+ of their supply. If more plants are hit, this becomes a humanitarian crisis on top of an energy crisis.

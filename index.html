🧠 What This All Means

This is a fear-driven selloff, not a structural one (yet). The market is pricing in a world where oil stays high because Iran isn’t resolving quickly, which means inflation stays sticky.

Friday’s weak jobs report created the worst possible combo: slowing growth + rising prices. That’s textbook stagflation — the macro environment that killed markets in the 1970s. The Fed is boxed in: can’t cut rates to help growth (because inflation is hot), can’t raise rates to fight inflation (because growth is weak).

The 10-Year yield dropping while oil surges is the market screaming ‘we’re scared.’ Money is flowing into Treasuries (safety) while commodities spike (fear of supply disruption). Gold at nearly $2,950 confirms the panic bid.

The Dow erasing all 2026 gains in a single week tells you how fragile sentiment is. One good Iran headline reverses everything. One bad one accelerates the selloff. We’re in headline-driven territory now.

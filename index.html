💡 WHAT THIS MEANS
THE OIL STORY

Today's crude crash from $119.48 to $84.86 is one of the most dramatic single-session reversals in oil history. Two forces are driving it:

BEARISH: G7 coordinated SPR release signals that the world's largest economies will actively intervene to cap oil prices. This removes the "uncapped upside" fear that was driving panic buying.
BEARISH: Kharg Island — Iran's main oil export terminal — was deliberately left untouched by US strikes. This suggests the conflict is being contained to military targets, not energy infrastructure.
STILL ELEVATED: At $84.86, oil is still +27% above pre-war levels ($67). The geopolitical risk premium hasn't fully unwound. Escalation could re-ignite the spike.
THE REAL RISK: WEDNESDAY CPI

Markets are fixated on oil today, but the February CPI print on Wednesday is the most important economic data release of 2026. Here's why:

STAGFLATION TEST: If CPI comes in hot while the economy is slowing under war pressure, it confirms the nightmare scenario — rising prices + falling growth. The Fed would be trapped.
FOMC MAR 17-18: The CPI reading directly feeds into the Fed's next rate decision. A hot print means no rate cuts despite the geopolitical shock. A cool print could open the door to emergency easing.
THE BULL CASE: If oil stays down near $85 and CPI is benign, markets could rally hard — the worst-case scenario (war + inflation + $120 oil) would be off the table.
BOTTOM LINE

The panic from this morning is fading. Oil's reversal + G7 SPR coordination + China mediation signals have pulled markets back from the brink. But we're not out of the woods. The real test is Wednesday's CPI. If inflation is running hot, the Fed can't ride to the rescue even if war escalates. Watch oil for re-acceleration — any strike on Kharg Island or Strait of Hormuz disruption would reset the clock to panic mode.

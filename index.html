🧠 What This Means — Analysis & Scenarios

🛢 Oil → CPI Pipeline

Oil at $116 feeds directly into Wednesday's CPI print. If inflation was already rising before this shock, stagflation is confirmed. Every $10 rise in oil adds ~0.3% to headline CPI within 2 months. This is the worst possible setup for the Fed.

🏭 Refinery Damage = Months

Destroyed refineries and storage facilities take months to repair — even a ceasefire tomorrow won't bring oil prices back quickly. The supply destruction is physical and real. Insurance markets for Gulf shipping are in freefall.

📉 Small Caps Crushed

Russell 2000 down -3.8% — hardest hit index. Small caps are the most vulnerable to the recession fears + oil shock combination. These companies can't hedge energy costs like megacaps. Expect more pain ahead.

🏦 The Fed Trap

Powell faces an impossible choice: cut rates (inflation surges higher) or hold rates (economy weakens into recession). Classic stagflation trap. Market pricing emergency cuts — but inflation data may force the Fed's hand the other way.

🔮 Three Scenarios

🕊 CEASEFIRE
Oil drops ~30% ($80-85 range). Markets rally 5-8%. VIX collapses. But refinery damage caps recovery speed. Best case: 2-3 month normalization.
🔥 ESCALATION
Oil $140+. S&P drops 15-20% from highs. Recession confirmed. Iran retaliates against Saudi infrastructure. Global energy crisis. Fed forced into emergency action.
⚖ STALEMATE
Oil range-bound $100-120. Markets grind lower with high volatility. Slow-burn stagflation. Fed holds rates. Most likely scenario for next 2-4 weeks.

What This Means

📖 The Big Picture — War, Oil, and the Inflation Bomb

Oil pulled back hard — from a $119.48 session high down to $100.85 after reports that Saudi Arabia is releasing partial strategic reserves. That's still +49% from pre-war levels. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively shut, Iranian refineries are ablaze, and Iraq's crude output is down 70%. This isn't over — it's a temporary reprieve.

The G7 emergency meeting signals this has moved from a regional military conflict to a global economic crisis. When G7 leaders convene specifically about oil prices, that's the system acknowledging the damage is structural, not transient. Expect coordinated strategic reserve releases and possible emergency measures.

Wednesday's CPI is THE number. If inflation was already rising before oil went parabolic, we're looking at a stagflation scenario — rising prices + slowing growth + a central bank that can't cut rates to stimulate because inflation is raging. This is the worst macro setup since the 1970s oil shock.

Equities are selling off broadly but not panicking — yet. The S&P is down ~1%, the Russell (small caps) is the worst performer at −1.66% reflecting domestic economic vulnerability. Crypto is bid — BTC +1.14%, ETH +2.90% — playing the "digital gold" role as a hedge against monetary policy uncertainty.

Bond prices are falling (yields rising), which means the market is pricing in higher inflation ahead rather than fleeing to safety. That's the stagflation signal — bonds aren't acting as a safe haven because the inflation threat is real. The FOMC meeting Mar 17-18 becomes a defining moment for Fed credibility.

⚠ KEY RISK: CPI Wednesday (Mar 11) + FOMC (Mar 17-18) — if CPI comes in hot AND the Fed can't cut, the stagflation trade accelerates. Oil above $100 for sustained periods historically precedes recessions.
💡 WATCH: Saudi oil release details, G7 emergency meeting outcomes, Hormuz shipping status. Any of these could swing oil ±$15 in a session. The $96-$120 WTI range is the new battlefield.

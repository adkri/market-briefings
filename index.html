🧠 Deep Dive — The Brent-WTI Spread — What $14+ Tells You

EDUCATIONAL

WHAT'S HAPPENING: Brent is at ~$110.80. WTI is at $96.09. That's a $14+ spread — normally it's $3-5. This morning, Brent briefly spiked to $119 while WTI barely moved. The spread is the single best indicator of war stress in the oil market.

WHY THEY DIVERGE: Brent prices oil that ships through global chokepoints — Hormuz, Suez, Bab el-Mandeb. WTI prices crude from Cushing, Oklahoma — landlocked, pipeline-connected, physically disconnected from the Gulf. When seaborne oil routes are disrupted, Brent spikes because DELIVERED oil costs more. WTI stays calmer because US production doesn't depend on Hormuz.

1990 GULF WAR PARALLEL: During the 1990 Gulf War (Iraq invades Kuwait), the Brent-WTI spread hit $11 — and Hormuz was never actually blocked. Today Hormuz IS blocked, LNG infrastructure is damaged, and 3 countries' energy was attacked in 24 hours. The $14+ spread is WORSE than any prior Gulf conflict. The market is pricing structural supply destruction, not a temporary blip.

WHO BENEFITS: Companies that buy at WTI and sell at Brent-linked prices capture the spread. Valero (VLO), Phillips 66 (PSX), Marathon Petroleum (MPC) — refiners buying cheap WTI crude, selling refined products at international prices. Enterprise Products (EPD), Plains All American (PAA) — crude export terminals capturing arbitrage. Cheniere Energy (LNG) — exports US LNG at global prices while buying cheap US gas.

PHYSICAL MARKET REALITY: Physical benchmark prices are even higher than Brent futures. Dubai/Oman crude, Murban, OPEC Basket — all trading at significant premiums. Brent futures at $110 are likely UNDERSTATING the real cost of delivered oil by $15-25. This gap typically closes with futures catching UP to physical.

THE CONVERGENCE TRADE: The spread always closes eventually — usually with futures rising to meet physical. In 1990, it took 3 weeks. In 2022 (Russia/Ukraine), spreads took 6 months to normalize. If the war continues and Hormuz stays blocked, Brent futures have room to rise to $130+. If ceasefire happens, both drop and converge at $85-90.

BOTTOM LINE: The $14+ Brent-WTI spread is bigger than 1990, bigger than 2008. Combined with Iran attacking 3 countries' energy in 24 hours, it's telling you this isn't a temporary disruption — it's structural supply destruction. The refiners (VLO, PSX, MPC) are the cleanest way to trade this spread.

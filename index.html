The Setup: Stagflation

Oil $67 → $119 in two weeks. Last time oil moved this fast was the 1973 Arab oil embargo. Oil hit $119 tonight — up 30.6% in a single session. 247 Wall St warns it could break the all-time record of $147/barrel this week.
Stock futures: NVDA -1.9%, GOOG -3%, TSLA -2%. Meanwhile Exxon +3.1%, Chevron +3.4%. A two-economy market: energy wins, everything else loses. Small caps (-3.8%) getting crushed because they can't absorb energy costs.
Wednesday's CPI is the hinge point: if February inflation was already rising BEFORE the oil shock, the Fed is trapped. They can't cut rates (inflation too high) and can't raise rates (economy already weakening). This is textbook stagflation — rising prices + falling employment.
Small caps (-3.8%) getting crushed because small companies can't absorb energy cost spikes like megacaps can. The Russell 2000 is the canary in the coal mine.
The dollar is weakening despite a war. This is unusual — normally war = dollar strength. Weakness suggests markets are pricing in US economic deterioration, not just geopolitical risk.
Gold holding near ATH at $5,186. Slight profit-taking but the safe haven trade is intact. The real move to watch: if gold breaks $5,200 this week, it signals institutional conviction that the crisis is deepening.

What This Means

Oil above $100 is a psychological and economic threshold. Last time was 2022 post-Ukraine invasion. The weekend gap from $91→$107 (+16.8%) is the largest single-session move in crude futures history. This isn't just war premium — actual infrastructure is burning. Destroyed refineries take months to rebuild even after a ceasefire.

We are now in a stagflation crisis. February payrolls: -92,000 jobs. Oil: $107. The textbook definition — rising prices + falling employment — is here. The Fed is completely trapped: cutting rates fuels inflation; holding rates deepens the recession.

Wednesday's CPI is the most important number of the month. It captures February inflation — before the oil shock. If inflation was already rising, it means $107 oil is pouring gasoline on an existing fire. If it was flat, there's at least some buffer. This data feeds directly into the FOMC decision on March 17-18.

Gold falling during a war is a bearish signal. -1.5% despite maximum uncertainty means institutional investors are selling gold to raise cash — likely for margin calls on equity positions. When the safe haven sells off, it means the pain in risk assets is forcing liquidation everywhere.

The dollar at 99.5 signals eroding confidence. In past wars, the dollar strengthened as a safe haven. This time it's weakening — suggesting markets view the US as the source of instability, not the refuge from it.

Three things to watch Monday: (1) Does oil hold above $100 or was the gap an overreaction? $100 is now the critical support/resistance level. (2) Does VIX break 30? That's the panic threshold. (3) Do we get any diplomatic signals from the G7 emergency meeting?

Why $108 Oil Changes Everything

Ras Laffan is offline. Energy infrastructure is being destroyed, not just threatened. There is no quick fix.

💰 At the pump: National average gas likely headed above $4.50/gal within days, potentially $5+ within weeks. Every $10 increase in oil ≈ $0.25/gal at the pump. That's a direct consumer tax — less spending on everything else.

The inflation problem: Oil at $108 flows into everything — transportation costs, plastics, chemicals, food production, heating. Even if core CPI was stable, headline inflation is about to spike. And if Wednesday's February CPI shows inflation was already running hot before the oil shock, the stagflation case becomes undeniable.

🏦 The Fed trap: The Federal Reserve is caught. Oil-driven inflation says "raise rates." A war-driven recession says "cut rates." They can't do both. The March 17–18 FOMC meeting will be one of the most consequential in years. Expect maximum ambiguity — the Fed will try to buy time.

Stagflation risk: Rising prices + slowing growth = stagflation. The last time the U.S. faced a genuine stagflationary oil shock was the 1970s. The playbook for that era doesn't work with modern debt levels. If this war drags on, we're in genuinely uncharted territory for post-2008 markets.

The energy map is being redrawn. Qatar's LNG offline means Europe loses its Russia-replacement plan. Asia loses its growth fuel. Russia sells at a premium. Iran's successor doubles down. Every week this war continues, the structural damage to global energy supply deepens — and that damage doesn't reverse when a ceasefire is signed.

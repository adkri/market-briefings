🧠 What This Means

ANALYSIS
🛢 $100 Oil = $4+ Gas Is Coming
The rough rule: every $10 increase in crude adds ~$0.25/gal at the pump. Oil has surged +$34/bbl since the war started (~$66 → $100). That translates to roughly +$0.85/gal at the pump, pushing national averages toward $4.00-4.25/gal. Some states will see $5+. This is the number that hits every American's wallet directly.
🏦 The Fed Is Completely Trapped
Oil at $100 is an inflation accelerant. It raises costs for everything: transportation, manufacturing, food, heating. The Fed can't cut rates into rising inflation — but a war-driven economic slowdown demands cuts. This is the textbook stagflation trap: rising prices + slowing growth + no good policy options. Powell's worst nightmare.
📊 Wednesday's CPI = The Most Important Number
February CPI (released Wed Mar 11) was collected before the worst of the oil shock. If it already shows rising inflation, the market will price in stagflation as baseline — because the oil-driven spike hasn't even hit the data yet. Hot CPI + $100 oil = potentially the worst macro setup since the 1970s. A cool CPI gives the Fed barely enough cover to stay patient.
⚖️ Stagflation Is Now the Base Case
With Hormuz blocked, Ras Laffan offline, and oil infrastructure being bombed, this isn't a temporary spike — it's a structural supply shock. The economy was already slowing under tariff uncertainty. Now add $100+ oil. Consumer spending compresses (gas takes wallet share from discretionary), corporate margins shrink (higher input costs), and inflation expectations un-anchor. The 1970s playbook is being re-run in real time.
💡 First Time Since 2022
Oil last touched $100 in mid-2022 during the Russia-Ukraine shock. That episode drove US inflation to 9.1% (June 2022 CPI). The difference now: the Fed already hiked rates to 5.25-5.50% to fight that inflation, then cut. Reversing course again would destroy credibility. The policy toolkit is exhausted. There is no clean exit.

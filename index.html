What This Means

🔥 Economic Warfare Phase

The conflict has crossed a critical threshold. With Israel striking Iranian oil infrastructure and Iran targeting Gulf desalination plants, this is no longer just a military campaign — it's full economic warfare. Civilian energy and water infrastructure are now legitimate targets for both sides. This guarantees prolonged commodity disruption regardless of ceasefire timing.

🇷🇺 Russia-Iran Intelligence Sharing = Proxy War

Russia providing Iran intelligence to target US forces transforms this from a regional conflict into a de facto US-Russia proxy war. This dramatically raises the escalation ceiling — any US casualties from Russian-sourced intel could trigger direct US-Russia confrontation. This is the single highest geopolitical risk factor right now.

🛢️ Oil $91+ — Gap Risk at 3 PM

WTI at $90.90 reflects Friday's close, but weekend escalation (Israeli oil depot strikes, Hormuz shutdown) hasn't been priced in. Expect a gap to $95+ when futures open at 3:00 PM PDT. The +35% weekly gain is already the largest in futures history. $100+ oil is now a realistic near-term scenario if Hormuz remains closed.

📊 Wednesday CPI = Most Important Number of the Month

February CPI drops Wednesday. Here's why it matters: this data predates the oil shock, capturing inflation before the war. If inflation is already rising → stagflation is confirmed before the energy crisis even hits the data. The March/April CPI prints (with $90+ oil baked in) will be devastating. Wednesday tells us how bad the starting point is.

🏛️ The Fed Is Trapped — FOMC Mar 17-18

Powell faces an impossible situation. Can't cut: inflation surging with oil at $90+ and supply chains disrupted. Can't hike: economy tipping toward recession with consumer confidence cratering. The March FOMC is the reckoning — expect the Fed to signal a long pause while markets price in stagflation. The dot plot will be the most-watched chart of the quarter.

⛽ LNG Crisis May Be Worse Than Oil

Qatar's Ras Laffan facility — the world's largest LNG exporter — has declared force majeure. Unlike oil, there's almost no spare LNG capacity globally. European gas storage is already low after winter. EU gas prices +60% in one week with no relief valve. This could trigger an energy crisis in Europe worse than 2022, with direct impact on EU industrial output and inflation.

🚫 No Exit Strategy Visible

Both the NYT and Washington Post are reporting no coherent endgame for the US air campaign. Iran says it "will not bow." Israel is escalating (hitting energy infrastructure, threatening successors). The Oslo embassy bombing shows the conflict is spreading beyond the Middle East. Without a diplomatic off-ramp, markets will continue pricing in prolonged disruption. Every day without ceasefire signals adds risk premium to every asset class.

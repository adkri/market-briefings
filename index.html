What This Means

The 2,100-Point Swing Is Historic

One of the largest intraday reversals in market history. Markets want to believe the war is ending. But Trump's track record on "mission accomplished" moments is mixed. The Dow going from -1,900 to +200 in a single session is the kind of move that happens at historic turning points — or at dead cat bounces. Which one is TBD.

Oil at $95 Is STILL a Crisis

Down from $119 but still +44% above pre-war levels. Every week above $90 feeds into CPI with a 4-6 week lag. Physical supply destruction doesn't reverse with a tweet. Iraq output is down 60-70%, the Strait of Hormuz is effectively closed, and even if a ceasefire happened tomorrow, it would take months to normalize supply chains.

Wednesday's CPI Is THE Number

February data captures prices BEFORE oil doubled. If inflation was already running hot pre-war, then stagflation is incoming regardless of war outcome. Energy costs haven't even begun filtering through to the data yet. A hot print would be catastrophic for the "Fed will save us" narrative. This is genuinely the most important number of the year.

The Reversal Is Based on Words, Not Actions

Iran's FM rejected ceasefire. The new Supreme Leader is more hardline than his father. Fighting continues. Israel is still bombing Tehran. Oil could re-spike on any escalation headline. The market is trading on a tweet and a phone call — not on any tangible change in the conflict.

Gold Selling During a War Was a Red Flag — Now Recovering

Gold selling during active conflict signaled margin call liquidation — funds selling everything to cover losses elsewhere. The slight recovery suggests forced selling may be easing. But gold at $5,085 near ATH with a war ongoing and oil at $95 tells you the market still sees massive uncertainty ahead. If CPI is hot Wednesday, gold could break to new all-time highs as stagflation hedging kicks in.

WHAT THIS MEANS — ANALYSIS

⛽ $5-6 Gas Is Here

Oil at $117/barrel — up 29% from Friday — translates directly to pump prices. National average already surging 14% in one week. Expect $5-6/gallon nationally within days. Some regions will see $7+. This acts as a regressive tax — hitting lower-income households hardest and crushing consumer spending.

📉 Stagflation Setup

The nightmare scenario is forming: rising prices + falling employment. February payrolls were already negative (-92K jobs). Oil at $117 ensures inflation will spike further. If Wednesday's CPI shows inflation was climbing before the oil shock, the Fed has no good moves. You can't cut rates to save jobs without pouring gasoline on inflation.

🔥 Wednesday CPI Is the Hinge

February CPI data on Wednesday is the most critical data point this month. It measures the period before oil spiked. If it's already hot → inflation was accelerating independently. If it's cool → this crisis is "just" an oil shock. Markets will move violently either way.

🏦 The Fed Is Trapped

FOMC meets March 17-18. They have no good options. Cut rates → risk runaway inflation from $117 oil. Hold rates → watch the labor market deteriorate further with -92K payrolls already. The "soft landing" narrative is dead. This is crisis management now.

🇶🇦 LNG Crisis May Be WORSE Than Oil

Qatar's Ras Laffan — the world's largest LNG facility — is offline under force majeure. Europe's gas storage was already declining heading into spring. If this drags on weeks, European natural gas prices could spike 200-400%, triggering a 2022-style energy crisis. This is underpriced.

⏳ Every Day Compounds Damage

Destroyed oil infrastructure doesn't come back quickly. Tehran refinery fires, Ras Laffan offline, Hormuz shipping disrupted, Kuwait & UAE cutting production — even a ceasefire tomorrow means months of repair. Every additional day of conflict multiplies the economic damage exponentially. Markets are pricing in weeks, not months.

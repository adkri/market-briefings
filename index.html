🧠 What This All Means

The oil spike IS the whole story. +12.67% in one day means the market just priced in a prolonged Iran disruption — not a quick resolution. When oil moves that fast, it triggers a chain reaction.

Oil spikes → inflation expectations rise → Fed can't cut rates. Higher energy costs → companies' margins compress → earnings estimates drop. VIX at 29.5 → options traders hedging aggressively → more volatility ahead. Gold at $5,181 → institutional money in 'protect capital' mode.

The fact that Asia was green while the US cratered tells you this was an intraday shock — the oil move accelerated during US hours. Monday's Asian session will be the first real test of whether this selloff goes global.

The combination of rising commodity prices + weak employment is textbook stagflation — the macro environment that killed markets in the 1970s. The Fed is boxed in: can't cut rates to help growth (inflation too hot), can't raise to fight inflation (growth too weak). March 18 FOMC is now the most important date on the calendar.

The crypto divergence is interesting — BTC holding flat while equities dump could mean crypto traders see this as temporary, or it could just be weekend-thin liquidity. Watch Sunday night for the real signal.

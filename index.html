🧠 Deep Dive: Why Asia Is Panicking

Asia sources 60% of its oil from the Middle East — far more than the US (11%) or Europe (25%). When Hormuz closes, it's existential for Asia, not theoretical.


What's happening right now:

  • 🇧🇩 Bangladesh: Fuel lines forming. Universities closed to save energy.
  • 🇮🇳 India: Emergency powers invoked to redirect LPG to households.
  • 🇵🇰 Pakistan: Schools closed, government services shifted online.
  • 🇰🇷 South Korea: First fuel price cap in 30 years.

Why the IEA release won't help Asia: The 400M barrel release is mostly from US and European reserves. Getting that oil to Asia requires tankers — which are the ones being attacked. The physical logistics of reserve releases assume functioning trade routes. Without Hormuz, the reserves can stabilize the West but do almost nothing for Asia.


Historical parallel: 1973 oil embargo hit Japan harder than anyone — they imported 99.7% of their oil. Japan responded by pivoting to nuclear and becoming the world's most energy-efficient economy. Watch for similar energy policy pivots across Asia if this crisis persists beyond 30 days.

Stagflation Setup

The macro picture is grim. Oil up 35% drives consumer prices higher while the economy loses jobs (−92k in February). Tariffs add a second inflation impulse. This is textbook stagflation: rising prices + falling employment simultaneously.

Fed Is Trapped

The Federal Reserve faces an impossible dilemma. Can't cut rates — inflation reigniting via energy prices and tariffs. Can't hike rates — economy already shedding jobs and war creating uncertainty. They'll likely freeze and buy time. Markets hate uncertainty more than bad news.

FOMC MAR 17–18 → EXPECTED: HOLD AT CURRENT RATE
📊

CPI Is the Hinge Point

Wednesday's CPI report (February data) is the single most important number this week. A hot print confirms stagflation fears and kills rate cut hopes — equities dump. A cool print gives the Fed room to cut if recession deepens — relief rally possible. Note: February CPI won't yet capture the full oil spike, but core readings will set the baseline.

🛢️

Supply Damage Persists

Even if a ceasefire materializes tomorrow, the damage is done. Destroyed refineries take months to years to rebuild. Ras Laffan (world's largest LNG facility) under force majeure. Strait of Hormuz shut. The LNG crisis may prove worse than oil — there is zero spare LNG capacity globally. Europe and Asia face energy supply crunches regardless of war outcome.

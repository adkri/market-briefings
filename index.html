▎💡 What This Actually Means
1
Oil at $100 = Everything Gets Expensive
$100 oil is a psychological AND economic barrier. Airlines cancel routes, trucking costs spike, and everything-that-moves-on-trucks gets more expensive. It's a tax on the entire economy.
2
Bonds Rising During Selloff = Stagflation
Normally when stocks crash, bond yields DROP (safety trade). Yields RISING means the market is saying the Fed can't cut rates because inflation is too hot. Worst of both worlds.
3
Russell −4.6% = Recession is Priced In
Small caps are domestic-focused — they can't absorb energy shocks. The Russell crashing hardest means the market is pricing in a US recession, not just volatility.
4
Gold Dropping During War = Liquidity Stress
Gold should rally in wars. When it drops, it means institutions are selling gold to meet margin calls on their stock losses. This signals real financial stress under the surface.
5
Wednesday's CPI = The Hinge Point
Hot CPI = stagflation confirmed, Fed trapped, markets panic. Soft CPI = Fed has room to cut, relief rally possible. This one number decides everything this week.
